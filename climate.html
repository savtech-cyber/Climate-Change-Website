Animal Extinction

Currently, Climate change is affecting 19% of endagered species (IUCN). Keeping species alive is incredibly important because we need to maintain our biodiversity. Biodiversity is incredibly important in supporting ecosystems by helping provide functions that supply oxygen, intake of carbon dioxide, clean water, pollination of plants, pest control, and many other ecosystem services.

For example The African antelope: By the year 2080, scientists predict that 82% of species of the African Antelope will see a decline in suitable habitat space due to climate change. Around 25% of the African Antelope packs will lose about half of their livable land due to this issue.

Sea Level Rise


Image showing sea level rise

Since the 1900s, sea level overall has risen from between 0.18 meters and 0.22 meters. Recent studies using a technology named accurate satellite altimeter shows that the current rate of sea-level rise is around 50% greater than that in the 1940s. Although many people think that the melting of glaciers and other ice forms are the main source of rising sea levels, it is actually driven by the thermal expansion of water. As the ocean warms, seawater becomes less dense and expands, rising sea levels. Zeke Hausfather stated in an article, “[t]he rapid increase in ocean heat content has led to around 19mm of sea level rise just from thermal expansion between 1993 and 2010, around a third of the total increase of 54mm.”

Sea level rise will have a extreme impacts on coastal cities and habitats from causing floods, erosion and contamination.

Ocean Acidification

Ocean acidification is when oceans are becoming more acidic, meaning that the pH lowers in the ocean. Ocean acidification is caused by CO2 gas in the atmosphere dissolving into the ocean. It is the changes in the chemical make-up of all the waters in the oceans. Many other factors contribute to rising carbon dioxide levels.

Ocean acidification negatively affects marine life because any water organisms with shells made from calcium carbonate will start to dissolve if oceans become more acidic. Shellfish and marine animals with shells rely on their shell for many things such as protection. If their shell dissolves they will die out affecting the food chain greatly.

Any steps that involve polluting less toxins into the air will help lesson ocean acidification. We can do any number of things, anything that lowers our Carbon Footprint.
